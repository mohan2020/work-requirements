
# Technical Blueprint: PA Medicaid Community Engagement Exemption System

This document serves as the master engineering design specification for the Penn Medicine Medicaid Community Engagement Exemption Application.

## Task 1: Clinical specifications, forms, and submission

### 1. Form requirements

Pennsylvania Department of Human Services (DHS) mandates specific documentation to exempt individuals from the monthly 80-hour work/community engagement requirement:

- PA Medical Frailty Self-Declaration and Provider Attestation Form
  - Section A (Patient): Self-declared physical or mental limitations, including contact details of treating providers.
  - Section B (Provider Attestation): Must be signed by a licensed physician, PA, CRNP, or clinical psychologist certifying that a physical illness, mental illness, or substance use disorder (SUD) significantly impairs the patient’s capacity to engage in 80 hours of monthly employment or community service.

- PA 1663 (Employability Assessment Form)
  - Used alternately if the patient is simultaneously qualifying for broader state cash assistance or Medicaid disability categories.
  - Requires checking "Permanently Disabled" or "Temporarily Disabled (for a duration of 12 months or more)" in Section II.

### 2. Clinical codes and multi-tier rules engine

Because Penn Medicine clinicians log conditions via SNOMED-CT in problem lists, while Pennsylvania DHS claims reviews rely strictly on ICD-10 billing codes, the platform must perform real-time translation and evaluation:

EHR Problem List (SNOMED-CT) -> UMLS Translation Engine -> ICD-10 Equivalence Map -> DHS Exemption Engine

#### Tier 1: Slam-dunk auto-exemptions

Patients matching any of the following parameters are automatically flagged as eligible, requiring only a one-click administrative signature confirmation.

| Category | ICD-10 billing codes | SNOMED-CT concepts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Serious Mental Illness (SMI) | F20.9, F31.9, F33.2 | 58214004, 13746004, 28475009 |
| Substance Use Disorders (SUD) | F11.20, F10.20 | 5602001, 28743005 |
| Active Oncological Conditions | C50.919, C18.9 | 254837009, 126852002 |
| End-Stage Organ Diseases | N18.6, I50.9 | 432241000124101, 85232005 |

#### Tier 2: Complex comorbidities and utilization metrics

Patients without a single Tier 1 condition are evaluated via combinatorial logic:

- Comorbidity threshold: presence of at least 2 complex chronic diseases, such as severe COPD J44.9 and insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus E11.9.
- Utilization proxy: the patient has experienced at least 2 emergency department visits or at least 1 inpatient admission within the trailing 12 months.
- Polypharmacy proxy: active outpatient medication profile containing at least 10 unique active prescription entries.

### 3. Submission pathways

- Administrative ex-parte cascades (automated): if the application prompts clinical staff to log a structured encounter during an ED or outpatient visit, the generated billing data is automatically pushed through the regional Health Information Organization (HIO) networks (P3N/HealthShare Exchange) directly to the state, achieving auto-exemption without manual paperwork.
- Digital upload (PA COMPASS Portal): pre-populated, clinician-signed forms are rendered as secure PDFs and routed directly to the patient’s Penn Medicine MyChart portal. The patient then uploads the signed PDF directly to their Pennsylvania COMPASS account.
- Physical/direct channels: printed forms are routed via automated fax server lines directly to the specific County Assistance Office (CAO) handling the patient’s case.

## Task 2: Data models, system APIs, and role framework

### 1. Relational schema design

To support non-real-time Databricks pipeline ingestion and real-time FHIR state synchronization, the following foundational data models are defined:

#### PatientRegistry
Tracks the Medicaid cohort and their exemption evaluation state.

- patient_id (VARCHAR, PK): unique EHR identifier
- mrn (VARCHAR): medical record number
- medicaid_id (VARCHAR): PA Medicaid recipient number
- first_name (VARCHAR) / last_name (VARCHAR)
- email (VARCHAR) / phone_number (VARCHAR)
- exemption_status (ENUM): UNASSESSED, ELIGIBLE_TIER_1, ELIGIBLE_TIER_2, EXEMPT_COMPLETED, NON_EXEMPT
- state_due_date (DATE): deadline for community engagement compliance

#### ClinicalExemptionScores
Saves the daily running analysis generated by the Databricks pipeline.

- score_id (UUID, PK)
- patient_id (VARCHAR, FK -> PatientRegistry)
- tier_classification (INT): 1 or 2
- matched_icd_codes (ARRAY[VARCHAR]): array of codes triggering the evaluation
- matched_snomed_codes (ARRAY[VARCHAR]): array of mapped problem-list codes
- utilization_count_12mo (INT): calculated inpatient/ED visit count
- medication_count (INT): unique medication count
- last_evaluated_at (TIMESTAMP)

#### ProviderAttestation
Records formal form generation, signing actions, and audit data.

- attestation_id (UUID, PK)
- patient_id (VARCHAR, FK -> PatientRegistry)
- provider_id (VARCHAR): NPI or EHR user ID of the signing clinician
- form_type (ENUM): PA_MF_ATT_B, PA_1663
- form_data_json (JSONB): fully serialized form parameters (answers, boxes checked, clinical summaries)
- signature_blob (BYTEA): secure vector or biometric signature data
- signed_at (TIMESTAMP)
- submission_status (ENUM): PENDING_PATIENT, SUBMITTED_COMPASS, FAXED_TO_CAO, AUTO_EX_PARTE

#### AuditLog
Keeps a strict log for compliance audits and tracking the 40% revenue at risk.

- log_id (BIGINT, PK)
- entity_id (VARCHAR): relevant patient ID or form ID
- actor_id (VARCHAR): user ID of clinician, CHW, or system process
- action_type (VARCHAR): REGISTRY_LOAD, FORM_GENERATION, PROVIDER_SIGN, PATIENT_NOTIFICATION
- timestamp (TIMESTAMP)

### 2. API specifications

#### FHIR and EHR integration APIs
The application leverages standard HL7 FHIR (R4) resources during clinical workflows to pull data and write back completed forms:

- GET /Patient/{id}: fetches demographics, insurance details, and contact preferences
- GET /Condition?patient={id}&clinical-status=active: pulls the EHR problem list and translates SNOMED terms
- GET /Encounter?patient={id}&date=ge{12_months_ago}: checks encounter volumes to evaluate Tier 2 utilization limits
- POST /DocumentReference: writes the completed, signed PA 1663/Attestation PDF back into Epic document storage under a dedicated document-type code

#### Custom application backend services
These APIs run on the custom backend layer connecting the application UI, Databricks pipelines, and the Epic frontend:

- GET /api/v1/registry/worklist
  - Description: returns a paginated list of patients matching search criteria, optimized for CHWs
  - Query parameters: status, assigned_chw, revenue_tier, page, limit
  - Response example:

```json
{
  "patients": [
    {
      "patient_id": "P910402",
      "name": "Jane Doe",
      "mrn": "100-200-300",
      "exemption_status": "ELIGIBLE_TIER_1",
      "score": { "tier_classification": 1, "matched_icd_codes": ["F33.2"] },
      "due_date": "2027-01-01"
    }
  ],
  "total_count": 4810
}
```

- POST /api/v1/forms/generate
  - Description: instantiates and pre-populates forms with clinical data pulled from Databricks
  - Payload: { "patient_id": "P910402", "form_type": "PA_MF_ATT_B" }
  - Response: serialized JSON payload representing the pre-populated inputs for UI rendering

- POST /api/v1/forms/sign-and-submit
  - Description: submits provider signatures, appends completed PDFs to Epic via FHIR, and initiates client communication
  - Payload:

```json
{
  "attestation_id": "a901-b203-c405",
  "signature_data": "data:image/png;base64,...",
  "provider_id": "NPI009381"
}
```

  - Response: { "status": "SUCCESS", "tracking_id": "SUB-991240" }

### 3. Role-based access control (RBAC)

The following roles partition access to clinical details, population worklists, and system administration:

| Role | Primary access |
| --- | --- |
| System Administrator | Configures rules and SNOMED mappings; manages integrations; views raw database logs |
| Clinician | Performs rapid ED/clinic attestation; reviews eligibility flags; applies digital signatures |
| Community Health Worker / Case Manager | Manages outreach worklists; logs outreach attempts; routes documents to CAOs |
| Compliance / Revenue Integrity Officer | Audits processes; reviews dashboards; monitors revenue impact |

- System Administrator (sys_admin): edit system rules engine; manage SNOMED-to-ICD-10 mapping tables; configure system integrations; view raw database logs
- Clinician (clinician): access the SMART on FHIR point-of-care app; view specific patient eligibility flags; review pre-populated forms; apply clinical digital signatures
- Community Health Worker / Case Manager (chw_user): read/write access to the population registry worklist; log patient outreach attempts; manually route patient documents to CAOs; manage worklist-level filters
- Compliance / Revenue Integrity Officer (compliance_user): read-only access to aggregated population dashboards; view financial impact charts; run performance audits on automated state ex-parte submissions
